Biography

Born on a boat. Coding since ten.

Founder, Oasis of Change, Inc. · Vancouver, BC · Building VCASSE.

I was born on a boat and taught myself to code at ten. By the time I turn twenty, I will have spent half my life writing software. I'm the founder of Oasis of Change, Inc., a Vancouver-based not-for-profit with the mission of promoting digital sustainability and accessibility. I'm also building the Vancouver Centre for AI Safety, Sustainability & Ethics (VCASSE), a subsidiary of Oasis of Change focused on the responsible development and deployment of AI systems.

I currently work with Plastic Bank to fight poverty and plastic pollution, and with AccessibilityChecker.org to help improve digital accessibility. I've also worked with the Stanley Park Ecology Society and Mittler Senior Technology on projects that have reduced website carbon emissions, energy use, and water use by up to 98%. I served as a National Coach (Fellow) with The Dais at Toronto Metropolitan University on their Screen Break program, Canada's first nationwide initiative for phone-free classrooms.

I'm a TEDx speaker on AI and digital sustainability, recipient of the 2025 Impact & Innovation Award from the City of Vancouver, and an alumnus of The Knowledge Society (TKS). Digital sustainability is heavily overlooked in mainstream tech conversations, and very few people my age are actively working in the field. I bring a perspective major events rarely get on stage.
