About

Born on a boat. Coding since ten.

I was born on a boat and lived there for six years. I taught myself to code at ten — by the time I turn twenty, I'll have spent half my life writing software. At fifteen I founded Oasis of Change, Inc. on the conviction that the web could be quieter, lighter, and more inclusive than the one we'd inherited.

Since then, my team and I have helped reduce website carbon emissions, energy use, and water use by up to 98% — for organizations like the Stanley Park Ecology Society and Mittler Senior Technology. We've supported nonprofits in securing more than $868,000 per year in in-kind technology grants, and helped plant over 17,000 trees in 32 countries.

I'm a TEDx speaker on AI and digital sustainability, recipient of the 2025 Impact & Innovation Award from the City of Vancouver, and an alumnus of The Knowledge Society (TKS). I've spoken with British Columbia's Minister of State for AI & New Technologies, and worked alongside Microsoft, IKEA, the West End Seniors' Network, and the Stanley Park Ecology Society.
