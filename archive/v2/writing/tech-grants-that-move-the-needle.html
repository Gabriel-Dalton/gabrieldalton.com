Tech grants for nonprofits — what actually moves the needle.

Notes from helping organizations across Canada secure more than $868,000/year in in-kind technology grants. Which programs are worth the application, which are not, and how to think about leverage.

Most tech grants for nonprofits are written about as if every program is a winning lottery ticket. They are not. Some are real, some are minor, and some will eat a month of your operations team's calendar in exchange for a feature your staff will never use. Here's the working list I've been refining.

The four programs worth almost any nonprofit's time.

Google Workspace for Nonprofits. Roughly $30,000/year in equivalent value. Almost every Canadian charity qualifies. The application is a few hours. The lift to email, calendar, and shared drives is enormous.

Google Ad Grants. $10,000/month in search ads. Materially game-changing if you have a clear "convert" action (donations, signups, registrations). It's worthless if you point it at a homepage and hope.

Microsoft for Nonprofits. Discounted Microsoft 365 plus Azure credits. The Azure side is what matters if you're running infrastructure — easily worth tens of thousands a year for a mid-sized org.

Canva for Nonprofits. The smallest of the four in dollar value, but the highest leverage in time saved per staff member. Free Pro for everyone on your team.

Three programs that get oversold.

  • Hardware grants. Useful for tiny grassroots orgs. Almost always less efficient than buying refurbished gear with operating funds and writing it off.
  • Marketplace credits from B2B SaaS vendors. Often look generous; usually expire before you can integrate.
  • "AI for Good" cohort programs. Genuinely high-effort. Worth it only if you'd already chosen to invest the staff time anyway.

How to think about leverage.

The dollar value on a grant is rarely the right number to optimise. The right number is "hours of staff time saved per quarter." A $30,000 Google Workspace grant that saves your three-person team five hours a week is worth more than a $200,000 platform credit you'll never use.

Optimise for hours saved, not dollars listed.

Common mistakes.

  1. Applying without a champion. Most of these programs require an internal owner. If nobody is going to actually use the tool, don't apply.
  2. Treating Ad Grants as a vanity metric. Ten thousand impressions a month is not a result. Five hundred completed donations is.
  3. Forgetting renewals. Several of these programs require annual re-attestation. Calendar it now.
